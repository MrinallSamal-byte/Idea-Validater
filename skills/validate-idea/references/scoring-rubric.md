# Scoring Rubric

Score the idea on five dimensions, 0–10 each, for a total out of 50. Score
honestly — a mediocre idea should land in the 20s. Inflating scores to be
encouraging defeats the entire purpose of this plugin. Give one line of
justification per dimension citing the specific reason for the number.

Anchor every score:
- **0–2** — fundamentally broken on this dimension.
- **3–4** — weak; a serious problem lives here.
- **5–6** — average; works but nothing special.
- **7–8** — strong; a real advantage.
- **9–10** — exceptional; rare, and you should be skeptical before awarding it.

## The five dimensions

### 1. Problem (0–10)
Is there a real, painful, frequent problem here — and does anyone actually have
it? Vitamins score lower than painkillers. A problem the user *imagines* others
have, with no evidence, caps at 4. Ask: who specifically feels this pain, how
often, and what do they do about it today?

### 2. Solution fit (0–10)
Does this solution actually solve that problem, and is it meaningfully better
than the current alternative (including "do nothing" and "use a spreadsheet")?
A 10x improvement on a narrow axis beats a 10% improvement on everything. Penalize
solutions in search of a problem.

### 3. Differentiation & moat (0–10)
Why does this win, and why can't an incumbent or a fast follower copy it next
quarter? "We'll execute better" is not a moat and scores ≤3. Look for real edges:
distribution, data, network effects, switching costs, regulation, unique insight.

### 4. Feasibility (0–10)
Can *this person/team* actually build and ship it with the resources implied?
Account for technical difficulty, capital, time, regulatory/legal hurdles, and
required expertise. A brilliant idea that needs a capability the team plainly
lacks scores low here — and that's important information.

### 5. Path to traction (0–10)
Is there a credible, affordable way to reach the first real users and get them to
adopt/pay? "If we build it they will come" scores ≤2. Strong answers name a
specific channel and why it works for this audience.

## Reading the total

- **40–50** — genuinely strong. Verdict likely *Pursue*. Still name the weakest dimension.
- **30–39** — promising with a real gap. Verdict likely *Pursue, but fix X first*.
- **20–29** — the kernel may be real but the current shape is wrong. Verdict likely *Reshape*.
- **10–19** — deep problems. Verdict likely *Park it* or *Reshape*.
- **0–9** — the load-bearing assumption is almost certainly false. Verdict likely *Drop it*.

The total is a summary, not the answer. A single 1 on a load-bearing dimension
(e.g. Problem) can sink an idea that scores well elsewhere — call that out
explicitly rather than hiding behind the average.
